city:Bangalore

Bangalore's old names include Bendakaluru (meaning "town of boiled beans") and Benga-val-oru ("City of Guards"), evolving from its origins as "Bengaluru," with the name Anglicized to "Bangalore" by the British, and officially restored to Bengaluru in 2014. Key Names & Origins Benga-val-oru: An ancient Kannada inscription from the 9th century refers to the area as this, meaning "City of Guards," indicating its historical significance. Bendakaluru: A popular legend links the name to a meal of boiled beans ("benda kal") offered to a lost king, evolving from "Benda-Kaal-Ooru" to Bengaluru and then Bangalore. Bengaluru: The city's native name, representing its Kannada roots, which was changed to Bangalore by the British for easier pronunciation. Bangalore: The Anglicized version used during British colonial rule, which became the common English name.
